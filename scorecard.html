Game formats explained

Not sure what to play? Here are the most common formats and how they work.

🎯 Stroke play

Count every stroke. Lowest total wins. The default — what tournaments use. Best for: any group size, any skill mix (with handicaps).

💰 Skins

Each hole is worth a "skin" (a dollar, a beer, whatever). Lowest score on the hole wins it. Tie = skin carries to the next hole. Big payouts when 3+ holes carry. Great with 3–4 players.

📊 Nassau

Three bets in one: front 9, back 9, and overall 18. Each is its own match. Often played with auto-doubles ("presses") when someone falls 2 down. Classic 4-ball format.

⚔️ Match play

Hole-by-hole, not stroke total. Win the hole = +1, lose = -1, tie = halve. The match ends when one side is up by more holes than there are left. Best 1-on-1 or 2-on-2 format.

👥 Scramble

Everyone tees off. Pick the best ball. Everyone hits from there. Repeat until in the hole. Friendly format — beginners love it because their bad shots don't count.

🎲 Wolf

4-player rotating "wolf" picks a partner each hole — or goes alone for double points. Strategy + scoring + trash talk. The most fun social format if you have a regular group.
