Warm up · Mental prep · Course management

Drop strokes before you swing

The simplest way to score better isn't a swing change — it's preparing well, picking the right club, and managing your misses. Here's how.

🧘 10-minute stretching routine

Most weekend golfers go straight from the car to the first tee — and pull a muscle on swing #1. This routine takes 10 minutes and prevents 90% of round-ending tweaks.

  1. Min 0–2 · Neck & shoulders

    Slow neck circles (5 each direction). Shoulder rolls forward and back. Arms across chest stretch (15 sec each side). Don't bounce — slow and controlled.

  2. Min 2–4 · Upper back & rotation

    Hold a club horizontally behind your shoulders. Rotate your torso left and right slowly, 10 reps. Then with arms extended overhead, lean side-to-side (10 each side).

  3. Min 4–6 · Hips

    Standing leg swings forward/back (10 each leg), then side-to-side (10 each leg). Standing hip circles. 90/90 hip stretch on a bench if available.

  4. Min 6–8 · Lower back & hamstrings

    Toe touches (slow, don't lock knees) — 10 reps. World's greatest stretch (a deep lunge with a torso rotation), 5 each side. Cat-cow on the grass if you don't mind kneeling.

  5. Min 8–10 · Wrists, forearms & warm-up swings

    Wrist circles, forearm stretches. Then 5 slow practice swings with a 7-iron, focused on rhythm — not power. Speed comes from being warm, not from swinging hard.

🏌️ The smart range warm-up

A typical bucket has ~50 balls. Most amateurs blast all 50 with the driver. That's the worst possible warm-up. Try this instead:

The sequence (50 balls, ~15 min)

  1. 5 wedge balls — half swings, just to feel rhythm
  2. 5 wedge balls — full swings
  3. 5 with a 9-iron
  4. 5 with a 7-iron
  5. 5 with a 5-iron or hybrid
  6. 5 with a fairway wood
  7. 5 with the driver
  8. 10 "play the round" — picture hole 1, hit your tee shot. Then hole 2's tee shot. Etc.
  9. 5 wedge balls to finish — back to rhythm and tempo.

Why this works

  • Starts with short, easy swings — warms muscles without strain.
  • Gradually builds to longer clubs — same path your back/hips need.
  • Ends on rhythm — not on a hooked driver.
  • The "play the round" portion gets your brain ready for actual shots, not range groove-finding.
Don't try to fix your swing on the range 20 minutes before a round. Rhythm only.

⛳ Putting green warm-up (5 minutes)

📏

Speed first, then aim

Drop 3 balls and putt them to the far edge of the green and back. Don't aim at a hole — just feel the speed. Repeat 2–3 times. Speed is 80% of putting.

🎯

Build the confidence circle

Drop 5 balls in a circle 3 feet from a hole. Make all 5 before walking off. If you miss, start over. Walk to the first tee believing you'll make every short putt.

🧠 Course management — drop strokes without changing your swing

The fastest way to score better is making smarter choices. These five rules alone will save 5–10 strokes a round for most amateurs.

  1. 1. Tee shot: aim at the widest part of the fairway

    Don't aim at the flag in the distance — aim at the safest landing zone. A ball in the rough is one stroke. A ball lost in the trees is two.

  2. 2. Approach shots: take more club than you think

    Amateurs leave 80% of their approach shots short. Most greens are protected in front (bunkers, water) and open in back. Aim for the middle of the green, not the flag — and take one more club.

  3. 3. Around the green: putt when you can, chip when you must

    If your ball is just off the green and the path is flat, putt it. The worst putt is almost always better than the worst chip. Use a high-lofted club only when you must clear something.

  4. 4. Bunkers: get out in one

    Don't try to hit the perfect shot from sand. Open the clubface, swing through the sand 2 inches behind the ball, and accept "anywhere on the green" as a win.

  5. 5. Putting: never leave it short

    "Never up, never in." A putt past the hole has a chance going in. A putt 2 feet short never does.

🎯 Mental game basics

🌬️

Reset between shots

You'll hit bad shots. Every golfer does. The pros don't avoid bad shots — they avoid follow-up bad shots. Take a deep breath, walk slowly, then commit to the next swing.

🧭

Pre-shot routine

Build a 20-second routine you do every shot: stand behind the ball, pick a target, take 1 practice swing, step in, look at target, swing. Same routine every shot — gives your brain something familiar when nerves rise.

📊

Score one hole at a time

Don't add up your score on hole 7. Don't think about "I'm on pace for a 92." Each hole is a fresh round. The only score that matters is the one on the next swing.

😤

Pick a "miss" before you swing

Decide which side is "safe" before you commit. Water on the left? Aim 10 yards right and accept the right miss. Bunker right? Aim left. Knowing your safe miss lets you swing freely.

The 80/80 rule: 80% of strokes lost by amateurs come from 80 yards and in. If you only have time to practice one thing, practice your wedges and your putting.
